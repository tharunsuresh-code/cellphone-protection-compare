## Purpose
A comparison tool for cell phone protection benefits across all major US credit cards.
Helps consumers find which credit cards cover phone damage/theft, compare deductibles,
claim limits, and identify which premium cards lack this benefit entirely.

## Content Summary
- 30 credit cards from 7 issuers (Amex, Chase, Wells Fargo, Capital One, US Bank, Barclays, Citi)
- Filterable by: issuer, annual fee, prepaid coverage, per-claim amount, deductible, card type
- Sortable table with pros/cons per card
- Section highlighting premium cards that do NOT include cell phone protection
- Prepaid coverage analysis: only Wells Fargo cards cover prepaid/MVNO plans

## Key Data Points
- Lowest deductible: $25 (Wells Fargo cards)
- Highest per-claim: $1,000 (Chase Ink Business Preferred)
- Most claims/year: 3 (Chase Ink Business Preferred)
- Best annual max: $3,000 (Chase Ink Business Preferred)
- Only prepaid-friendly issuer: Wells Fargo (no exclusion clause)
- Premium cards WITHOUT protection: Amex Platinum, Chase Sapphire Reserve, Amex Gold,
  Chase Sapphire Preferred, Citi Strata Premier, Bilt, Capital One Venture,
  Chase Ink Business Preferred (in this context), Capital One Spark Miles

## Technology
- Single HTML file, no dependencies, no build step
- Vanilla HTML/CSS/JavaScript
- Light/dark mode toggle with localStorage persistence
- Hosted on GitHub Pages

## Data Freshness
Credit card benefits change frequently. Always verify current terms with the card issuer
before filing a claim. If data is outdated, please open an issue or PR on GitHub.
