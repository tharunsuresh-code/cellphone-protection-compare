The Prepaid Problem

Most issuers require you to pay a "monthly cellular service bill" with your card, which typically excludes prepaid, pay-as-you-go, or MVNO plans (Mint Mobile, Google Fi, etc.) — even though carriers like AT&T Prepaid and T-Mobile Prepaid do generate monthly bills with autopay.

Wells Fargo is the exception. Their cell phone protection benefit guide does not include a prepaid exclusion clause, making Wells Fargo cards (Autograph, Active Cash, Reflect) the best option for prepaid plan users. The Wells Fargo Autograph ($0 fee, $25 deductible) is the top pick.

Chase, Amex, Capital One, US Bank, and Barclays all require a "monthly cellular service bill" and will deny claims on prepaid plans. Chase Freedom Flex does not cover prepaid despite being frequently recommended elsewhere — claims will be denied.
